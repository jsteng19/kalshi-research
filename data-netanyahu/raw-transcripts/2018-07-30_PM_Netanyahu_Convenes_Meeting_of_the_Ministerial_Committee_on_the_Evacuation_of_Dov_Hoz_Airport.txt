The ministerial team on the evacuation of Dov Hoz Airport, chaired by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, met today, at the Prime Minister's Office in Jerusalem, for a discussion which was attended by Finance Minister Moshe Kahlon, Transportation and Road Safety Minister Yisrael Katz, Tourism Minister Yariv Levin, head of the Prime Minister's Staff Yoav Hurvitz, Prime Minister's Office Director General Eli Groner, Finance Ministry Director General Shai Babad, Finance Ministry Budget Director Shaul Meridor, Attorney General Avichai Mandelblit and other senior officials. Appearing before the committee were Eilat Mayor Meir Yitzhak Halevy, Tel Aviv-Yafo Mayor Ron Huldai, Airports Authority Director General Yaakov Ganot, Israel Lands Authority Director Shimron Adiel and Israel Planning Administration Director Dalit Zilber.

It was determined that Eilat has national priority and is a national resource of great importance. Prime Minister Netanyahu instructed the relevant parties at the discussion to give preference to Eilat residents and visitors regarding the allocation of flights from BGI Airport upon the closure of Dov Hoz.

In order to provide for the accelerated growth in the number of tourists arriving in Israel, Prime Minister Netanyahu directed that a plan be advanced to build an airport on an artificial island adjacent to the coast of the State of Israel.
