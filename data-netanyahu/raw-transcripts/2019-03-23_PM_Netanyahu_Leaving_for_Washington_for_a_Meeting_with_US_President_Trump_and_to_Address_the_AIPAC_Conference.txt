Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, this evening, will leave for Washington DC. He will meet with US President Donald Trump at the White House on Monday. The two will meet again on Tuesday over dinner. Among the topics Prime Minister Netanyahu will discuss with President Trump are the Iranian aggression, Iran's efforts to entrench militarily in Syria, how to prevent Iran from attaining nuclear weapons and the tightening of cooperation on security and intelligence matters. Prime Minister Netanyahu will stay at Blair House, the official guest residence of the US President. Prime Minister Netanyahu will address the annual AIPAC conference on Tuesday morning. He will then hold a series of diplomatic meetings on Capitol Hill with Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, the Senate leadership and Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer. The Prime Minister will also meet with House of Representatives Speaker Nancy Pelosi, the House leadership and House Minority Leader Kevin McCarthy. Prime Minister Netanyahu: "I am leaving for Washington tonight to meet with President Trump. I will thank him for the American recognition of Israeli sovereignty over the Golan Heights. I will discuss with him the continuation of our joint struggle against Iranian aggression. Our steps will only increase."
