Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, today, chaired a discussion with the chairmen of the open-air market committees in order to formulate an outline for the gradual reopening of the markets. It was agreed that the Prime Minister's Office (PMO) – in cooperation with the Economy and Industry Ministry, the Health Ministry, the National Security Council (NSC) and open-air market committee representatives – would formulate an outline to gradually reopen the markets subject to regulation of entry to the market areas and maintenance of the rules of social distancing as per the 'purple badge' standard rules. Also attending the discussion were Economy and Industry Minister Eli Cohen, Jerusalem Mayor Moshe Leon, the head of the Prime Minister's Staff Asher Hayon, NSC head Meir Ben-Shabbat, PMO Director General Ronen Peretz, Health Ministry Director General Moshe Bar Siman Tov, Finance Ministry Director General Shai Babad, National Economic Council head Prof. Avi Simhon and other senior officials.
