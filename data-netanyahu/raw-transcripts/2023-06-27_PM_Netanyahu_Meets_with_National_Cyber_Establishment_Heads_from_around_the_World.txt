Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, yesterday evening, at ISA headquarters, met with cyber establishment heads from around the world, together with ISA Director Ronen Bar and Israel National Cyber Directorate (INCD) Director General Gaby Portnoy.

Prime Minister Netanyahu said that a decade ago he set a goal of turning Israel into one of the leading countries in cyber-defense, and that this goal has been met. He emphasized that continued success in the cyber struggle also depends on the international cooperation that Israel is leading, and commends its success.

Attending the meeting were cyber-defense delegations from some ten countries that have come to Israel to attend the CyberWeek conferencebeing sponsored by the INCD and the TAU Blavatnik Interdisciplinary Cyber Research Center, as well as National Security Council Director Tzachi Hanegbi, Prime Minister's Chief of Staff Tzachi Braverman and Prime Minister's Military Secretary Maj.-Gen. Avi Gil.
