Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, today, on the sidelines of the UN General Assembly in New York, met with Malawian President Lazarus Chakwera and South Sudanese President Salva Kiir.

Prime Minister Netanyahu said that he is determined to strengthen ties with the countries of Africa and increase cooperation in innovation, agriculture, food security and water. The Prime Minister expressed Israel’s readiness for cooperation that could contribute to African stability and prosperity.

Prime Minister Netanyahu commended the African Union’s accession to the G20 as a permanent member.

The Prime Minister invited the two leaders to visit Israel and both replied that they would be pleased to accept the invitation.
