Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu today in Jerusalem, met with a delegation of 20 ambassadors based in Washington, the visit of whom is being sponsored by The Israel Project. The Prime Minister called on them not to support a unilateral Palestinian move at the UN which is liable to severely damage the diplomatic process.Prime Minister Netanyahu told the ambassadors that, "Support for this draft decision is liable to severely harm the possibility of resuming the direct negotiations and advancing the peace process. A unilateral UN decision would prevent their leadership from making the concessions in the future that are necessary for peace."Participating in the delegation are the ambassadors [to the US] from Slovakia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Montenegro, Albania, Macedonia, Burkina-Faso, Uganda, Liberia, Benin, Barbados, Dominica, Grenada, Haiti, Belize, St. Lucia, Trinidad and Tobago, the Dominican Republic, Mongolia and East Timor, most of which have no permanent representation in Israel.
