Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, this evening, directed that the ISA be integrated into the war on the crime families in the Arab sector. The directive was issued at the end of an emergency meeting convened by Prime Minister Netanyahu to evaluate bringing the ISA into the war on the crime families. During the discussion, the positions of the ISA, the Israel Police and the Justice Ministry were presented, as were those of the ministers. At the conclusion of the discussion, Prime Minister Netanyahu directed National Security Council Director Tzachi Hanegbi to formulate operative recommendations to be submitted at a follow-up discussion next week. A ministerial committee on Arab sector affairs, to be chaired by the Prime Minister, will also be established. Following the meeting, Prime Minister Netanyahu spoke with Maher Khaliliah , the Chairman of the Yafia Local Council, where five people were murdered last Thursday and expressed his condolences, adding that the government was determined to fight the crime families in order to restore quiet to the Arab sector. Also participating in the meeting were Justice Minister Yariv Levin, National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir, ISA Director Ronen Bar, National Security Council Director Tzachi Hanegbi, Prime Minister's Chief of Staff Tzachi Braverman, Cabinet Secretary Yossi Fuchs, Prime Minister's Military Secretary Maj.-Gen. Avi Gil, Attorney General Gali Baharav-Miara, Israel Police Inspector General Koby Shabtai, the ISA Deputy Director, the head of the ISA Israel Dept., the ISA Legal Adviser, the National Security Council Legal Adviser and State Attorney Amit Isman.
