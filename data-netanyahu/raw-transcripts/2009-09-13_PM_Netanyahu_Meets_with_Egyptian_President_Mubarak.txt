Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak met today in Cairo in order to discuss recent and future developments in the Middle East as well as a wide range of bilateral, regional and international issues. The talks were productive and were held in a friendly atmosphere.

Prime Minister Netanyahu and President Mubarak discussed the challenges facing the region and the need by Israel, the Palestinians, the Arab states and the international community to do their part in advancing the peace process.

The two leaders also discussed the situation in Gaza and ways to strengthen security. Prime Minister Netanyahu thanked President Mubarak for Egypt's assistance and efforts to advance the release of abducted soldier Gilad Shalit.

Prime Minister Netanyahu and President Mubarak expressed their desire to work towards advancing peace in the region.
