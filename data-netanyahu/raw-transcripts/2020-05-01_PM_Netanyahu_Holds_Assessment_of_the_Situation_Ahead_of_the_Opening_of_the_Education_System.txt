Prime Minister Netanyahu held a consultation to assess the situation regarding the opening of the education system at which the logistical-administrative readiness of the education system and the [local] authorities, updated morbidity data, the findings of the Gertner report on infection among children and the situation in other countries, were considered.

Pursuant to the discussion that was held on 27 April 2020 and the conditions that were set therein, an additional discussion and assessment were held today. It was decided to resume the activity of the education system while meeting all of the sanitary conditions set by the Health Ministry, as follows:

* On Sunday, 3 May 2020, five grades in general education and five in ultra-orthodox education, will return to class.

General education – Grades 1-3 and 11-12

Ultra-orthodox education – Ages parallel to grades 7-11

Special education pupils will fully return to classes.

Youth at risk – Hours will be agreed on separately

* Next week, logistical-administrative preparations will be completed for daycare and kindergartens; it is intended to return them to activity on Sunday, 10 May 2020. An assessment of the situation will be held before their return.

* Regarding grades 4-10, an outline will be formulated regarding a return to activity in May and no later than 1 June 2020.

* At this stage, classes will not be defined as mandatory (except for matriculation examinations).

* The Education Ministry will be required to verify that education institutions have the means to ensure that they meet the hygiene and sanitary conditions.

* The decision will be updated within the framework of a public health order by the Director General of the Health Ministry.

Education Minister Rafi Peretz, Interior Minister Aryeh Deri, Labor, Social Affairs and Social Services Minister Ofir Akunis, Health Minister Yaakov Litzman, Finance Minister Moshe Kahlon, National Security Council head Meir Ben-Shabbat, Prime Minister's Office Director General Ronen Peretz, Education Ministry Director General Shmuel Abuav, Health Ministry Director General Moshe Bar Siman Tov, Health Ministry Public Health Service Director Prof. Sigal Sadetzsky and other senior officials attended the consultation.
