Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, today, met with Hungarian Foreign Affairs and Trade Minister Peter Szijjarto and told him, "We have a great relationship, great friendship. I want to talk to you about how to further improve our relations."

The two discussed improving bilateral economic cooperation in automotive technologies and the automotive industry, energy, water and academia. The Prime Minister expressed his appreciation for the good and friendly relations between the two countries and thanked the Hungarian Foreign Affairs and Trade Minister for his country's support for Israel in various forums.

Prime Minister Netanyahu noted the importance of enhancing relations with the Visegrad Group countries at a meeting to be held during his visit to Hungary this July.
